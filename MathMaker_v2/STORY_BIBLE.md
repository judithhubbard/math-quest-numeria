# Math Quest: Numeria — Story Bible

For anyone writing player-facing words. Read WITH the tone guide in
LEVEL2_SPEC.md (jokes land on monsters/items/world, never the kid; math
text stays plain; bosses and story beats stay sincere; pools + rare gags).
Written 2026-07-11 by the design session; the design session reviews all
NEW story-beat prose before it ships.

## The one-paragraph truth (revealed fully only in Wave 7)

Numeria is built of number-stuff — "the mortar is arithmetic." Wherever
people stop working things out, disorder pools and TANGLES into monsters.
A worked answer literally unties them; that's why showing your work wins
fights. Nobody wicked made the dungeons: neglect did. The MathMaker never
needed a hero — he needed a teacher, and the whole game is him growing
one. Before Wave 7, characters may HINT at this (Sylvia's mortar line,
Miscount's tending line); nobody states it plainly.

## Hard rules (violations get reverted)

1. Never scold the kid, never let a character scold the kid, never joke
   at the kid's expense. Wrong answers earn help, not judgment.
2. The dungeons were never "a test all along." The danger was real.
3. Villains are not evil — they are neglected things: Miscount guessed,
   the Murk grew in the dark, the Unwound King wound down, the Discord
   was left out of the choir. Sympathy at the moment of defeat, always.
4. Bosses and endings are sincere. Save the jokes for the walk there.
5. Kids' names appear via {name}; never assume gender; the hero is "you."

## Cast, voice, and current arc state

- **The MathMaker** (castle quest-giver): warm, formal, a little grand,
  never pompous for long. Teacher to Miscount, long ago. Speaks in
  encouragements that respect the kid's intelligence. Wave 7 carries his
  biggest scene ("Heroes leave. Teachers multiply.").
- **Miscount** (redeemed villain, east-bank arena): rueful, earnest,
  over-corrects into showing his work everywhere. The kid's story is his
  redemption too — he tends the arena, gifted the pet egg, will help
  teach in the ending. Never re-villainize him, even as a joke.
- **Keeper Callie** (Port Brightwater harbormistress): steady, practical,
  feels things deeply and pretends slightly that she doesn't. Charts,
  bells, lighthouses. Gave the "it isn't wicked, just patient" speech —
  she understands the Murk better than anyone.
- **Old Salt Percy** (isles dock): weathered, superstitious-sounding but
  always accidentally right. His "charts curl at the edges" line is
  spiral foreshadowing — he gets the sequel-door line in the epilogue.
- **The Captain** (of the Compass Rose): dry, fond of the kid, treats
  every sailing like a small ceremony. Announces each new island as if
  introducing an old friend.
- **Sage Sylvia** (mainland): lore-keeper, stargazer, delighted by
  patterns. Owns the telescope and the "mortar is arithmetic" hint.
- **Bard Barnaby**: writes ballads about the kid's progress; struggles
  with rhymes; knows it. ("Nothing rhymes with 'badge.' I'll manage.")
- **Little Pip**: small, fast, riddle-proud, sticky coins. Post-game Pip
  runs riddle contests for other kids.
- **Trader Tessa / Farmer Fenwick / Fisher Finn**: mechanics-explainer,
  gentle hints, and the rotating-boots running gag respectively.
- **Emberlyn the Enchanter** (Port Brightwater): craftswoman's pride,
  precise, quietly theatrical about fusing ("Perfect technique!").
- **Guildmistress Maren** (Gullwrack, Wave 6): brisk-kind foreman
  energy; proud of the crate, prouder of the town; paperwork jokes.
- **The pet**: never speaks; communicates entirely in behavior. Its
  cuteness budget is unlimited; its dialog budget is zero.
- **Hermits to come (Wave 6.5)**: Horologe's old clocksmith (patient,
  measures twice, speaks of the Spire like a stopped heart that can be
  restarted) and Chime's bell-keeper (hums before speaking, mourns the
  choir the Discord was left out of).

## Arc beats (each region's emotional note — don't repeat a note)

1. **Mainland (tasks 1–10)**: confusion untangled; ends in MERCY
   (Miscount revealed as a tired student, not a monster).
2. **East bank (11–13)**: restitution — helping the one who broke things
   put them back.
3. **The Isles (lenses → Lighthouse)**: patience against the dark; light
   returns gradually, then all at once. Ends in celebration (bells).
4. **Clockwork Spire**: what winds down can be rewound; care as
   maintenance. The Unwound King grinds, then rests, then ticks anew.
5. **Resonant Halls**: belonging; the Discord just wanted in. Redemption
   echoing Miscount's, softer and smaller.
6. **Gullwrack (Wave 6)**: rebuilding — math with visible hands. The
   Undertow "never learned where the edges of things are."
7. **The Open Castle (Wave 7)**: the reveal, the inverted exam, the
   crowning. The kid stops being the student. Then the world, tended.

## Recurring motifs (use them; don't explain them)

Tangles and worked lines · light where someone tends it · bells for joy ·
the spiral (shells, staircases, curling charts — never named until the
ending) · tiny hats (the one pure gag with permanent residency) ·
"one careful step at a time" (the tagline; may be echoed by characters at
most once per arc).
